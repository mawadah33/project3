Symptoms:

You may have a color vision deficiency and not know it.
Some people figure out that they or their
child has the condition when it causes
confusion such as when there are problems differentiating the
colors in a traffic light or interpreting color-coded learning materials.

People affected by color blindness may not be able to distinguish:

  • Different shades of red and green
  • Different shades of blue and yellow
  • Any colors

The most common color deficiency is an inability to see some shades of red
and green. Often, a person who is red-green or blue-yellow deficient isn't completely
insensitive to both colors. Defects can be mild, moderate or severe.

When should you see a doctor:

When you notice a change in color vision.

Perform an eye examination.

Before starting color blindness tests, the doctor must conduct an eye examination
to look for poor vision, neurological problems, glaucoma, or other eye problems and ensure the safety of vision and the safety of all parts of the eye.

Color blindness test:

The color blindness test is a visual test composed of panels called pseudochromatic panels. These panels contain
images colored dotly in one of the colors and within the color a number or symbol embedded in the image. Only a person with normal vision can distinguish
color and symbol together, while a color-blind patient may see the number without the color or the color without the number or symbol.
It is important for children to be tested before they start school because many early childhood educational subjects involve identifying colours.
